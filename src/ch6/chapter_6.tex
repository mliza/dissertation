This work has touched the surface of the multyphysics nature of \gls{AO} in
hypersonic flows. This work has used state of the art open source solvers (SU2)
to investigate the effect of \gls{AO} in a hypersonic flow. This work has
showed that the chemestry composition is importnat because the polarizablity
will be affected by the chemestry composition of the gas. It has also showed
that for hypersonic flows, the \textit{frozen} flow assumption is not good
enough, and that nonequilibrium effect have to be considered. This work has
also shwod that for the cases investigated a $5$ species gas is a good
approximation, in practice \gls{AO} effects become importnat at low mach
numbers and low altitudes (terminal); and ioniation is importnat at high mach
number sand high altitudes (\cref{fig:ch2flightSpeedAltitude}). In practices
for \gls{AO} applicatins, this is not a regime of big concern.
