A commonly used model to describe nonequilibrium effects is the Park $2T$ model
\cite{Park1989}, which assumes that the flow can be described by two
temperatures. One temperature $\Parenthesis{T_{tr}}$ will describe the heavy-particle translational
and molecular rotational energy and the other temperature
$\Parenthesis{T_{vr}}$ will describe the molecular vibrational, electron
translational, and electronic excitation energies.
