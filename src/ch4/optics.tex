\section{Optics\label{ch4:optics}}
    \Cref{fig:kerlAirPolarizabilityT} shows the results of curve fitting developed
    by Kerl \cite{Kerl1992}. This fitting uses constants defined in
    \cref{tab:kerlConstants}. These constants are accessible in the
    \textit{constants} module from the \gls{HAOT} package and results match
    published results by Kerl \cite{Kerl1992}.
    \begin{equation}\label{eq:kerlFitting}
        \alpha(\omega,T) = \frac{\alpha(0,0)}{1 -
        \Parenthesis{\frac{\omega}{\omega_0}}^2} \Parenthesis{1 + bT + cT^2}
    \end{equation}
    
    \begin{table}[H]
        \makebox[\linewidth][c]{
            \begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|} 
                \hline
                Molecule & $\alpha_0\times10^{-30}\Units{m^3}$ &
                $\omega_0\times10^{16}\UnitsFrac{rads}{sec}$ &
                $b\times10^{-6}\UnitsFrac{1}{K}$ &
                $c\times10^{-9}\UnitsFrac{1}{K}$ \\
                \hline\hline
                $\ce{N2}$ & $1.7406$ & $2.6049$ & $1.8$ & -- \\ \hline
                $\ce{O2}$ & $1.5658$ & $2.1801$ & $-2.369$ & $8.687$ \\ \hline
                $Air$ & $1.6970$ & $2.47044$ & $10.6$ & $7.909$ \\ \hline
            \end{tabular}
        }
            \caption{Kerl fitting constants \cite{Kerl1992}
            \cref{eq:kerlFitting}}\label{tab:kerlConstants}
    \end{table}

    \Cref{fig:haotN2buldakov,fig:haotO2buldakov} show the comparison between
    the \gls{HAOT} package and the results published by Buldakov
    \cite{Buldakov2000}. There is a treant between the \gls{HAOT} and Buldakov
    results but it is not a one to one match. The Buldakov approach used many
    constants \ref{tab:polarizabilityDerivatives}, but these constants are not
    properly explained. This method is based on an expansion from the center
    and the first term of the expansion with the polarizability found in open
    literature.
