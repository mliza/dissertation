%\subsection{\acrfull{LES} Study\label{sec:lesStudy}}
This section investigates \gls{AO} properties in a turbulent flow under thermal
and chemical equilibrium conditions. \Cref{tab:lesStudy} summarizes the flow
conditions used in this investigation. The $1L$ case described in
\Cref{tab:lesStudy} was validated by Tumuklu \cite{Tumuklu2024} through
comparison of \gls{CFD} results with experimental data. The purpose of this
section is to develop \gls{HAOT} capabilities for turbulent flows. A
low-enthalpy case is used, and \gls{AO} is investigated as a function of
temperature. The $2L$ case described in \cref{tab:lesStudy} has not been
validated; but the solver has been validated at subsonic speeds
\cite{Molina2017}.

    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}
            \hline
            Case & Solver & $M\Units{\;}$ & $T_{\infty}$ &
            $P_{\infty}\Units{Pa}$ & $Re\Units{\;}$  \\ 
            \hline \hline
            $1L$ & OpenFOAM & $0.4$ & $288.0$ & $101.3\mathrm{E}{3}$ & $1.6\mathrm{E}{6}$ \\ \hline
            $2L$ & SU2 & $5.0$ & $298.0$ & $851.1$ & $9.4\mathrm{E}{5}$ \\ \hline
        \end{tabular}
    \caption{Flow properties \label{tab:lesStudy}}
    \end{center}
\end{table}
