\chapter*{Abstract}
Hypersonic flows present unique challenges to measurements made by optical
imagining sensors due the complex interplay between fluid dynamic,
thermodynamic, and chemical reactions on the atmosphere surrounding the sensor.
In particular, the thermal non-equilibrium and turbulent nature of the flow
field can cause both a deflection and a possibly a degradation to the optical
signal as it traverses the boundary layer. A physical understanding of the
impact of these phenomenon on the optical signal is necessary to accurately
assess sensor performance. While previous research in aero-optics has
predominantly focused on low-speed applications with perfect gas assumptions,
this work aims to investigate the effect on an optical signal in the presence
of a turbulent flow and in a real gas environment, that is not in thermodynamic
equilibrium. Three cases will be examined: (i) low enthalpy, low-flow
resolution; (ii) low enthalpy, high-flow resolution; and (iii) high enthalpy,
low-flow resolution. At present, the author is currently using the simulation
(SU2), in additin an Open-Source tool HAOT was created to analyze
aero-optic properties for aero-optic applications. All results presented on
this work were perform with HAOT.
