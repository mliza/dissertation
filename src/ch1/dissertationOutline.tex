\section{Dissertation Outline\label{ch1:dissertationOutline}}
\Cref{ch:ch2} will present the fundamental theoretical background
necessary to understand the field of aero-optics, including key physical
concepts, governing equations, and relevant literature. This chapter will
establish the foundation for the subsequent analysis by reviewing prior
research and highlighting gaps that this dissertation aims to address. \\

\Cref{ch:ch3} will focus on the computational fluid dynamics
(\gls{CFD}) tools used to perform the simulations. It will provide an overview
of the selected solvers, numerical methods, and turbulence models. \\

\Cref{ch:ch4} will introduce the High-fidelity Aero-Optic Tool
(\gls{HAOT}) developed for this research. This chapter will detail the main
functionalities of \gls{HAOT}, including its implementation, governing
equations, and computational framework, demonstrating how it extends existing
aero-optic analysis capabilities. \\

\Cref{ch:ch5} will present case studies that combine the \gls{CFD}
results with the \gls{HAOT} tool to analyze key aero-optic phenomena. This
chapter will explore the impact of turbulence, nonequilibrium effects, and
refractive index variations on optical wave propagation in hypersonic flows. \\

Finally, \cref{ch:ch6} will summarize the key findings of this
dissertation, discussing their implications for aero-optical analysis in
hypersonic regimes. It will also outline potential future research directions
based on the results obtained. \\
