 \chapter*{Abstract}
Hypersonic flows present unique challenges to measurements made by optical imagining sensors due the complex interplay between fluid dynamic, thermodynamic, and chemical reactions on the atmosphere surrounding the sensor. In particular, the thermal non-equilibrium and turbulent nature of the flow field can cause both a deflection and a possibly a degradation to the optical signal as it traverses the boundary layer. A physical understanding of the impact of these phenomenon on the optical signal is necessary to accurately assess sensor performance. While previous research in aero-optics has predominantly focused on low-speed applications with perfect gas assumptions, this work aims to investigate the effect on an optical signal in the presence of a turbulent flow in a real gas, that is not in thermodynamic equilibrium. Four cases will be examined: (i) low enthalpy, low-flow resolution; (ii) low enthalpy, high-flow resolution; (iii) high enthalpy, low-flow resolution; and (iv) high enthalpy, high-flow resolution. At present, the author is currently using the simulation (SU2), which is not capable of investigating cases (ii) and (iv). To perform an accurate assessment of these four cases will require the development of a new model, which is the focus of this investigation.
